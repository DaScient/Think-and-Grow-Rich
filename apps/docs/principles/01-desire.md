# 1. Desire — The Starting Point of All Achievement

> *"The starting point of all achievement is desire. Keep this constantly in mind. Weak desires bring weak results, just as a small fire makes a small amount of heat."* — Napoleon Hill

## What It Is

Desire is not a wish. It is an **obsessive, burning demand** of the mind that commands the subconscious to work ceaselessly toward its fulfillment. Every great achievement begins as a thought — a single definite idea held with such intensity that it becomes an irresistible force.

## Key Insight

A weak desire produces weak results. A burning, obsessive desire becomes the unstoppable fuel of achievement.

## The Six Steps for Transmuting Desire

::: tip Action Steps
1. **Fix in your mind** the exact amount of money or goal you desire. Be precise — no vague intentions.
2. **Determine exactly** what you intend to give in return for the money or goal.
3. **Establish a definite date** by which you will possess your desire.
4. **Create a definite plan** for carrying out your desire and begin at once, ready or not.
5. **Write out a clear, concise statement** of your goal, the date, your exchange, and your plan.
6. **Read your written statement aloud** twice daily — once upon rising, once before retiring.
:::

## The Edwin C. Barnes Story

Barnes had a burning desire to go into business with Thomas Edison — not to work *for* him, but *with* him. He had no money, no qualifications, no connections. He boarded a freight train to Menlo Park anyway.

Years later, Barnes became Edison's business partner for the Edison Dictating Machine, earning a fortune. The desire did not waver — it simply transformed into faith, then into action.

::: warning The Darby Lesson
R.U. Darby stopped three feet from a gold vein. When he quit, the man who bought his equipment found the vein immediately. Darby later turned this defeat into his greatest lesson, building a successful insurance career on the conviction: **never quit**.
:::
