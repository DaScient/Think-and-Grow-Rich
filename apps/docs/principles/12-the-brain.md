# 12. The Brain — A Broadcasting and Receiving Station for Thought

> *"The brain is a broadcasting and receiving station for thought. Every human brain is capable of picking up vibrations of thought released by other brains."* — Napoleon Hill

## What It Is

The human brain is both a transmitter and receiver of thought vibrations. When stimulated by strong emotion — particularly desire and faith — the brain operates at an elevated rate of vibration that increases its capacity to both broadcast thoughts powerfully and receive the creative thought impulses broadcast by other minds.

The Master Mind operates partly through this mechanism: the minds of the group, stimulated into harmony, broadcast and receive at elevated frequencies, creating a "superconscious" capacity that no individual possesses alone.

## Key Insight

Creative imagination is the receiver of thoughts broadcast from other minds. The more stimulated and disciplined the brain, the clearer the reception.

## The Three Steps

::: tip Action Steps
1. **Stimulate your brain** through strong positive emotion — particularly desire and faith — before your most important mental work.
2. **Engage regularly with brilliant, stimulating minds** through your Master Mind group — exposure elevates your own broadcast frequency.
3. **Practice focused, sustained attention** — a scattered, distracted mind is a poor transmitter and receiver of creative intelligence.
:::
