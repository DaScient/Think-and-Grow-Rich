# 7. Decision — The Mastery of Procrastination

> *"Successful people reach decisions promptly and change them slowly, if ever. Unsuccessful people reach decisions slowly, if at all, and change them often."* — Napoleon Hill

## What It Is

Procrastination — the opposite of decision — is a common enemy that every person must conquer. Analysis of over 25,000 individuals revealed that the habit of prompt, definite decision-making is one of the most consistent characteristics of successful people.

## Key Insight

Procrastination is the opposite of decision. Every day you delay a necessary decision, you feed the enemy of achievement.

## The Four Steps

::: tip Action Steps
1. **Decide promptly** when all available facts are at hand — do not wait for perfect information.
2. **Once decided, defend your decision** against those who would undermine it through doubt or criticism.
3. **Keep your plans private** — sharing unformed plans with others plants seeds of doubt and invites unsolicited opinion.
4. **Change decisions only** when new objective evidence demands it — not from emotional wavering or social pressure.
:::

## The Pattern of the Great

| Trait | Successful People | Unsuccessful People |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Deciding | Quickly | Slowly |
| Changing | Slowly | Frequently |
| Source | Own reasoning | Others' opinions |
| Action | Immediate | Delayed |

## The Master Key

Henry Ford decided to put the V-8 engine into production. Engineers said it was impossible. He told them to stay until they found a way. They stayed. The engine was built.

Decision, backed by desire and persistence, dissolves impossibility.

## Daily Affirmation

*"I am a decisive person. I gather the facts, trust my judgment, decide promptly, and act with conviction."*
